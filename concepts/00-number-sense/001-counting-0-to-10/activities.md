# Hands-on Activities: Counting 0 to 10

These off-screen activities use the Concrete-Pictorial-Abstract (CPA) model to lock in number foundations. Play these physical games before or alongside the digital child activity.

---

## Game 1: The Move-Over Grocery Shop (Concrete Stage)
* **Goal**: Solve partitioning failures by physically moving counted items.
* **Items needed**: A toy shopping bag or basket, and 10 small pantry items (apples, onions, small boxes, tin cans).
* **Setup**: Place all 10 items on one side of the table (the "shop shelf").
* **How to play**: 
  1. Tell your child: *"We need to buy exactly 6 items for dinner today."*
  2. Have your child pick up one item at a time, count it aloud, and place it inside the basket: *"One (place in bag)... Two (place in bag)... Three..."*
  3. Stop them when they reach 6.
  4. Ask: *"How many items did we buy?"* Encourage them to answer *"6"* immediately without emptying the bag to count again.

---

## Game 2: Sidewalk Hopscotch Counting (Physical / Rhythmic Stage)
* **Goal**: Match movement pace with number recitation.
* **Items needed**: Sidewalk chalk.
* **Setup**: Draw a row of 10 large squares on the ground, numbered 1 to 10 in order.
* **How to play**:
  1. Stand at the beginning, before square 1 (this is **zero**!).
  2. Have your child jump into square 1 and say *"One"*.
  3. Jump into square 2 and say *"Two"*.
  4. If they jump faster than they say the words, hold them back. They must stay in the square until they speak the number clearly.
  5. Play backward: hop backward from 10 to 1, counting down to zero!

---

## Game 3: The Hidden Cup Challenge (Abstract Cardinality Stage)
* **Goal**: Build cardinality and conservation of number.
* **Items needed**: 5 small plastic cups, and 5 small coins or marbles.
* **Setup**: Line up the 5 cups on the table.
* **How to play**:
  1. Place 1 coin in front of each cup. Count them together: *"1, 2, 3, 4, 5."*
  2. Now, put the coins under the cups so they are hidden.
  3. Ask: *"How many coins are under the cups?"* 
  4. If they immediately say *"5"*, they have mastered cardinality. If they hesitate, lift the cups, count them again, hide them, and repeat.
  5. Expand by hiding 3 coins and leaving 2 cups empty to build subtraction basics.
