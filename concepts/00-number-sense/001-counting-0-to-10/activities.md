# Activities: Counting 0 to 10

Here are off-screen hands-on games to play with your child to strengthen their counting skills.

---

## Activity 1: The Treasure Hunt (Off-screen)
* **Goal**: Count items found around the house.
* **How to play**: Give your child a small bowl and say: "Can you find and bring back 7 small stones?" or "Bring me 4 crayons." Once they bring them back, count them together by laying them out in a straight line.

---

## Activity 2: The Action Counter (Physical)
* **Goal**: Match movements to verbal count.
* **How to play**: Say: "Let's do 5 hops!" or "Let's do 8 claps!" Count each hop or clap together as you do them. This connects physical rhythm to numbers.

---

## Extension Challenge: The Mix-up (Advanced)
* **Goal**: Show that shape arrangement doesn't change count.
* **How to play**: Put 6 cups in a straight line and count them. Now, scatter them randomly on the floor. Ask: "How many are there now without counting?" See if they realize it's still 6 (conservation). If they count again, that is perfectly normal; keep repeating in different patterns.
