# Common Misconceptions: Counting 0 to 10

Here are the most common mistake patterns to watch for when a child counts to 10:

---

## 1. Rushing/Asynchrony
* **The Mistake**: The child recites the number words faster or slower than they touch the items. For example, they might say "1, 2, 3, 4" while only touching 2 apples.
* **The Conceptual Break**: Lacking one-to-one correspondence between the word said and the physical object.
* **How to help**: Guide their finger to touch the item as they say the word, slowing down their speed.

---

## 2. Double Counting or Skipping
* **The Mistake**: The child counts an item twice, or skips an item entirely.
* **The Conceptual Break**: Failing to keep track of which items have already been counted and which ones have not.
* **How to help**: Have the child move the counted items from one pile to another as they count.

---

## 3. Order-Irrelevance Confusion
* **The Mistake**: Thinking that the number of objects changes if they count from right-to-left instead of left-to-right.
* **The Conceptual Break**: Not realizing that the arrangement of objects does not affect the total quantity (conservation of number).
* **How to help**: Arrange a set of 5 blocks, count them left-to-right, then right-to-left to show they both equal 5.
