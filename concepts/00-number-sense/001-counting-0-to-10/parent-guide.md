# Parent Guide: Counting 0 to 10

This guide helps you teach your child how to count items up to 10. Follow the steps below to connect this digital game with real-world learning.

---

## ⏰ The 2-Minute Concept Explanation
Counting is the bridge between the physical world and abstract numbers. A child might easily recite "1, 2, 3, 4, 5" like a song, but counting a set of objects requires matching each spoken word to exactly one physical object (one-to-one correspondence). 

The most important milestone here is **cardinality**: understanding that the last number spoken is the total count of the group. If you count "1, 2, 3, 4" and ask "How many are there?", and the child recounts "1, 2, 3, 4" instead of answering "4", they are still developing cardinality.

---

## 🎲 The 5-Minute Everyday Activity (Concrete Stage)
**Items needed**: 8 small snacks (cereal pieces, grapes, crackers) or toys (Lego bricks, coins).
1. Place 6 pieces of cereal in a straight line on the table.
2. Have your child touch each piece with their index finger, counting aloud: *"1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6."*
3. **The Cardinality Check**: Cover the line of snacks with your hand and ask: *"How many did you count?"* Listen for an immediate answer: *"Six!"*
4. Encourage them to eat the snacks one by one, counting down to zero!

---

## 🚀 The 10-Minute Extension Challenge (Pictorial / Spatial Stage)
**Items needed**: 8 toy blocks or cups.
1. Place 6 blocks in a neat row. Count them together (6).
2. Now, scramble the blocks into a messy cluster right in front of them.
3. Ask: *"How many blocks are here now?"* 
   - If they immediately say *"Six!"* without counting again, they have mastered **Conservation of Number** (the count doesn't change when shape changes).
   - If they start counting them one by one again, that is perfectly normal! Guide them through it. Ask: *"Does rearranging them make blocks appear or disappear?"*

---

## 🔍 Common Mistake Diagnosis & Correction
- **Mistake**: Child says number names faster than their finger touches objects (e.g. reciting "1, 2, 3, 4, 5" while only pointing to 3 cups).
  - *Correction*: Place your hand over their hand. Gently guide their index finger to touch each cup, pausing on each one as you say the number together.
- **Mistake**: Child double-counts an item or skips over one.
  - *Correction*: Set up a "counted zone". Have them physically move each block from the left side of the table to the right side as they count: *"Move 1... move 2... move 3..."*

---

## 🚫 What NOT to Do
- **Do NOT point for them**: Let them use their own finger. The physical touch-connection between their finger and their voice is what builds the cognitive link.
- **Do NOT rush them**: If they take a long pause between "four" and "five", let them think. Speed is not a measure of primary math mastery.
- **Do NOT show frustration**: If they get the count wrong, do not say "no, that's wrong." Say: *"Let's check together! Let's touch them one more time."*

---

## 🎯 When to Move to the Next Concept
Move on to **Counting 10 to 20** or **Compare More, Less, Equal** once your child can:
1. Count up to 10 scattered items without skipping or double-counting.
2. Answer "how many?" immediately with the correct total.
3. Correctly match the count of a set to a written numeral.
