# Parent Guide: Counting 0 to 10

## What this concept means
Counting is not just reciting numbers (like "1, 2, 3..."). It is the ability to match each number name to exactly **one** object (one-to-one correspondence) and understand that the last number said is the total count of the objects (cardinality).

## Why it matters
This is the absolute foundation of all mathematics. If a child cannot count objects up to 10 reliably, they will struggle with addition, subtraction, place value, and general problem-solving.

## How to teach using everyday objects at home
- **Cereal Counting**: Place 6 pieces of cereal on the table. Have your child touch each piece as they say the number: "1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6."
- **Stair Steps**: Count the steps aloud as you walk up the stairs together.
- **Toy Sort**: Count toy cars or blocks as they put them back into their toy basket.

## Questions to ask
- "Can you show me 5 fingers?"
- "How many apples are on the table?"
- "If I add one more, how many will there be?"
