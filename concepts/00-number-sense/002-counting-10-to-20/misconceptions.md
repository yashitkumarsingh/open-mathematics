# Common Misconceptions: Counting 10 to 20

Teen numbers represent a major milestone and bring unique learning challenges:

---

## 1. Confusing "Teens" with "Ties"
* **The Mistake**: The child confuses names like "thirteen" and "thirty", or "fourteen" and "forty".
* **The Conceptual Break**: Misinterpreting the pronunciation suffix, leading to confusion between smaller teen values (13) and larger tens values (30).
* **How to help**: Exaggerate the "teen" sound when counting. Emphasize that "teen" numbers always start with a "1" in the tens place.

---

## 2. Reversing Digit Placement
* **The Mistake**: The child writes "14" as "41", or "17" as "71".
* **The Conceptual Break**: Writing the digit they hear first (e.g., they hear "seven-teen", so they write 7 first, then 1).
* **How to help**: Practice writing the "1" first while saying: "Ten and seven more make seventeen. The ten comes first!"

---

## 3. Counting Breakdowns above 12
* **The Mistake**: The child counts smoothly to 12, but then starts reciting random numbers or slips back to 1.
* **The Conceptual Break**: The pattern names change from distinct labels ("eleven", "twelve") to teen suffixes.
* **How to help**: Sing number songs from 10 to 20, reinforcing the sequence verbally.
