# Parent Guide: Counting 10 to 20

## What this concept means
Counting up to 20 expands a child's number sense into the teens. Teen numbers are difficult because their English names do not follow a simple pattern (e.g., "eleven", "twelve", "thirteen" instead of "one-ten", "two-ten", "three-ten"). The goal is for the child to recognize that teen numbers are made of a group of "ten" and some more.

## Why it matters
Understanding teen numbers sets the foundation for place value (tens and ones), base-ten counting, and adding double-digit numbers later.

## How to teach using everyday objects at home
- **Double Handfuls**: Place a group of 15 beans on a table. Help the child bundle 10 of them first, then count the remaining 5: "10 and 5 make 15."
- **Egg Carton Counting**: Use a 12-egg carton and add a small dish with 4 more items to represent 16.
- **Visual Groups**: Draw a full set of 10 circles and then single circles next to it to count.

## Questions to ask
- "What number comes after 12?"
- "Can you count out 14 Lego blocks?"
- "Is 17 closer to 10 or 20?"
