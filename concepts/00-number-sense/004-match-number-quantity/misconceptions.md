# Common Misconceptions: Match Number to Quantity

Recognizing digits and linking them to counts involves several conceptual steps:

---

## 1. Visual Digit Confusion
* **The Mistake**: The child confuses similar-looking digits like 6 and 9, or 2 and 5.
* **The Conceptual Break**: Visuo-spatial processing. The child knows the numbers but cannot distinguish the graphical shapes.
* **How to help**: Trace the digits in sand or on paper, calling out the strokes: "Start at the top, curve around for 6."

---

## 2. Cardinality Fail
* **The Mistake**: The child counts 5 toys correctly (1, 2, 3, 4, 5) but when handed cards with numerals 3, 5, 8, they pick a random card.
* **The Conceptual Break**: Lacking understanding that the final count (5) maps to the numeral. They treat the counting action as separate from the written number.
* **How to help**: Keep the objects in place, write the numeral "5" right next to the fifth object, and point to both.
