# Parent Guide: Match Number to Quantity

## What this concept means
Matching number to quantity is connecting abstract symbols (the written numerals 0–10, like "4") to a physical or visual collection of items (four dots 🟠🟠🟠🟠).

## Why it matters
Children often learn to count verbally like a song first, without understanding that the symbol "6" refers to exactly 6 objects. Linking digits to quantities is the core bridge between physical blocks and written equations.

## How to teach using everyday objects at home
- **Card Match**: Write numbers 1 to 5 on cards. Hand the cards to your child and ask them to place the matching number of stones or toys on each card.
- **Plate Dice**: Draw different numbers of dots on paper plates (e.g. 3, 5, 2). Write numbers on stickers or blocks and have the child sort them to the correct plate.
- **Find the Room**: Say: "Can you bring me 3 socks?" and then write down the number 3 together on a piece of paper.

## Questions to ask
- "Which of these groups matches the number 5?"
- "Can you count out these blocks and find the card with the right number?"
- "Why does this card match these 4 cars?"
