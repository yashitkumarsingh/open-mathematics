# Common Misconceptions: Ten Frame Basics

Understanding the 2x5 grid layout requires visual sorting:

---

## 1. Disorganised Filling (No Anchors)
* **The Mistake**: The child fills the ten-frame randomly (e.g. placing counters in the corners first).
* **The Conceptual Break**: Failing to see the frame as a tool for structuring numbers. Random placing makes it hard to subitise (instantly recognize) 5 and 10.
* **How to help**: Encourage the child to fill the top row from left to right first. Say: "Let's fill the top shelf first!"

---

## 2. Empty-Space Confusion
* **The Mistake**: The child counts empty squares instead of dots, or includes empty squares in their count of total items.
* **The Conceptual Break**: Lacking focus on the objects as the target (one-to-one correspondence) vs. the background grid.
* **How to help**: Practice counting the dots, then count the empty spaces separately. Show that "dots + empty spaces = 10".
