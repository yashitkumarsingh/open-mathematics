# Parent Guide: Ten Frame Basics

## What this concept means
A ten-frame is a 2x5 grid that helps children visualize quantities. Instead of counting dots one-by-one, children learn to see relationships (e.g. they see that a full top row is exactly **5**, and a full frame is exactly **10**).

## Why it matters
The ten-frame builds subitising (the ability to instantly know the count without counting) and structures numbers around the key anchors of 5 and 10. This prepares children for addition, subtraction, place value, and regrouping (e.g. 8 + 6 is 10 and 4 more).

## How to teach using everyday objects at home
- **Egg Carton Ten-Frame**: Cut a standard egg carton so it has exactly 10 compartments (2x5). Place plastic counters, cereal pieces, or buttons in the spots.
- **Tape Grid**: Tape a 2x5 grid on the floor. Have your child jump into squares as you call out numbers, placing a toy in each square.
- **Top Row Rule**: Teach your child to always fill the top row from left to right first before starting on the bottom row. This builds a visual anchor for 5.

## Questions to ask
- "How many more dots do we need to make 5?"
- "If the top row is full, how many dots do we have?"
- "Can you show me 7 on the ten-frame?"
