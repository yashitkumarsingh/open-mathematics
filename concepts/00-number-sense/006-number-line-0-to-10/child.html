Number Line 0 to 10

🏠 Parent Dashboard

💡 What this concept means

A number line represents numbers as locations along a straight path. It shows children that numbers have a fixed order, and that as they move to the right, values increase. This transitions children from counting objects (discontinuous quantities) to measuring length (continuous quantities).

❓ Common Misconceptions to watch for

  • Line vs. Space: Counting the tick lines instead of the hop intervals (spaces). Guide them to make actual jump movements, only naming the number upon landing.
  • Zero skip: Skipping the zero point and counting the first tick as 0. Help them see that the line starts at 0.

🏡 Off-screen Hands-on Activities

  • Chalk hops: Draw a chalk line on the pavement from 0 to 10. Ask your child to stand at 0 and jump: "Jump 3 hops! Where did you land?"
  • Ruler bug crawls: Take a plastic bug or toy figure and walk it along a standard ruler, counting each centimeter mark together.
