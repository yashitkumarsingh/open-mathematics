# Common Misconceptions: Number Line 0 to 10

Number lines require measuring distances rather than discrete collections:

---

## 1. Counting Lines vs. Spaces
* **The Mistake**: The child counts the notch tick-marks starting from 0 as "1", landing on the wrong count. E.g. they start at 0, touch the first notch (0) and say "1".
* **The Conceptual Break**: Treating the ticks as objects to count rather than measuring the intervals (steps/spaces) between the numbers.
* **How to help**: Use physical hops. Tell the child: "A hop starts at 0. You don't count until you land on the next line!"

---

## 2. Order Reversal or Random Gaps
* **The Mistake**: The child writes number lines with irregular gaps (e.g. putting 3 right next to 4, but leaving a giant space between 4 and 5).
* **The Conceptual Break**: Not realizing that numbers on a number line represent equal units of distance.
* **How to help**: Use blocks of identical size (like Duplo/Lego) placed end-to-end to build a physical number line, showing that each step is exactly one block wide.
