# Parent Guide: Number Line 0 to 10

## What this concept means
A number line represents numbers as physical locations along a straight path, indicating distances from zero. It shows that numbers have a fixed order and that as you move to the right, the numbers get larger.

## Why it matters
The number line is one of the most powerful models in mathematics. It transitions a child's understanding from counting individual objects (discrete sets) to measuring continuous length. It is crucial for understanding fractions, addition as "hopping right", and subtraction as "hopping left".

## How to teach using everyday objects at home
- **Chalk Line**: Draw a line from 0 to 10 on the pavement outside. Write the numbers. Have your child jump to each number as you count: "Hop to 4! Now hop one step forward to 5!"
- **Ruler Walks**: Use a standard ruler. Place a tiny toy (like a toy bug or lego figure) and walk it along the inch or centimeter lines.
- **Missing Card Line**: Write numbers 0 to 10 on cards. Lay them out in order, but hide 1 or 2 cards face down. Ask your child to guess the hidden numbers.

## Questions to ask
- "Which number comes just before 4?"
- "What number is between 7 and 9?"
- "If I start at 3 and take 2 hops forward, where do I land?"
