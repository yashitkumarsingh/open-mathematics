# Common Misconceptions: Number Bonds to 5

Understanding part-whole splits of 5 involves structural counting:

---

## 1. Confusing "Parts" and "Wholes"
* **The Mistake**: When asked "If you have 3, how many more to make 5?", the child clicks or selects "5".
* **The Conceptual Break**: Confusing the target sum (the whole, 5) with the missing piece (the part, 2). They hear the number "5" and think they need to represent that value rather than solve the relation.
* **How to help**: Use concrete objects. Say: "We have 3 blocks. Let's add blocks one by one until we have 5. How many did we add?"

---

## 2. Rote Memorisation without Visual Sense
* **The Mistake**: The child can chant "3 and 2 is 5", but when shown a drawing of 3 blue birds and 2 red birds, they cannot identify the parts.
* **The Conceptual Break**: Memorizing language labels without anchoring them to visual partitions.
* **How to help**: Use two-color counters or split diagrams to constantly connect the facts to shapes.
