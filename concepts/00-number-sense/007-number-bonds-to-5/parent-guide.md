# Parent Guide: Number Bonds to 5

## What this concept means
Number bonds represent the relationship between a whole number and the parts that combine to make it. For the number 5, the combinations are:
- 5 and 0
- 4 and 1
- 3 and 2

## Why it matters
Number bonds lay the foundation for mental arithmetic. If a child knows by heart that 3 and 2 make 5, they do not need to count on their fingers to solve `3 + 2` or `5 - 3`. This structural fluency is essential for speed and accuracy in addition and subtraction.

## How to teach using everyday objects at home
- **Fist Game**: Put 5 small stones in your hand. Shake them, then hide some in your left hand and some in your right hand. Show the child one hand (e.g. showing 3 stones) and ask: "If I have 3 here, how many are hidden in my other hand to make 5?"
- **Split plate**: Draw a T-chart on a paper plate dividing it into a top "whole" section and two bottom "part" sections. Place 5 pretzels in the top section, then split them into the bottom parts (e.g. 4 and 1).
- **Five-Frame Coloring**: Draw a grid of 5 squares. Use two colors to color the squares (e.g. 2 blue and 3 yellow) to show different combinations.

## Questions to ask
- "If I have 4 cookies and want 5, how many more do I need?"
- "What are two numbers that put together make 5?"
- "If I have 5 blocks and take away 2, how many are left?"
