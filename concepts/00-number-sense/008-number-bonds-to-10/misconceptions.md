# Common Misconceptions: Number Bonds to 10

Recognizing combinations that sum to 10:

---

## 1. Bridging-Five Breakdown
* **The Mistake**: The child knows bonds to 5 easily (like 3+2), but when solving for 10 (like 8 + ? = 10), they guess random numbers.
* **The Conceptual Break**: Failing to extend part-whole thinking beyond the single-hand count (5) to the double-hand count (10).
* **How to help**: Use a ten-frame grid constantly. Show that the bottom row represents the extension of numbers past 5.

---

## 2. Hand-Counting Errors
* **The Mistake**: The child folds fingers down to solve bonds of 10 but miscounts the folded fingers, getting off-by-one errors (e.g. saying 8 + 3 = 10).
* **The Conceptual Break**: Lacking motor coordination or track-keeping when folding multiple fingers.
* **How to help**: Use physical objects on a grid rather than fingers, so the items are fixed in place and easy to count.
