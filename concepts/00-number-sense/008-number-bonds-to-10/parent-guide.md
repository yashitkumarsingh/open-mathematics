# Parent Guide: Number Bonds to 10

## What this concept means
Number bonds to 10 are pairs of numbers that combine to make exactly 10 (e.g. 9+1, 8+2, 7+3, 6+4, 5+5). Visualizing these on a ten-frame shows that the empty spaces represent the missing part.

## Why it matters
Fluency with combinations of 10 is the single most important skill for multi-digit addition and subtraction. For example, when adding `8 + 6`, children learn to "make a ten" by splitting 6 into `2` and `4`, calculating `8 + 2 = 10` and then `10 + 4 = 14`.

## How to teach using everyday objects at home
- **Ten-Frame Fills**: Draw or tape a ten-frame. Put 8 red apples in the squares. Ask: "How many empty spots are left? So 8 and what makes 10?"
- **Ten Fingers**: Hold up all 10 fingers. Bend down 3 fingers. Ask: "How many fingers are standing? (7). How many are bent? (3). So 7 and 3 make 10!"
- **Hanger & Pegs**: Take a coat hanger and place 10 clothes pegs on it. Split them into two groups (e.g., 6 on the left, 4 on the right).

## Questions to ask
- "If I have 7 crayons, how many more do I need to fill a box of 10?"
- "What is the partner number for 8 to make 10?"
- "We have 10 blocks. If 6 are blue, how many are red?"
