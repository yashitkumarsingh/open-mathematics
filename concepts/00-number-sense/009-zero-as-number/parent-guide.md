# Parent Guide: Zero as a Number

## What this concept means
Zero is a highly abstract concept. It is not just "nothing"; it is the mathematical representation of an empty set. Children need to understand that "0" is a number that counts the absence of objects.

## Why it matters
Recognizing zero is essential for understanding place value (e.g. in 10, the 0 indicates zero ones), subtraction (e.g. 5 - 5 = 0), and starting point positions on a number line or ruler.

## How to teach using everyday objects at home
- **The Cookie Box**: Show a box containing 3 cookies. Eat them all. Show the empty box and ask: "How many cookies are left now? (Zero!)."
- **Hide the Toy**: Place a cup over a toy. Lift the cup and show the toy is there. Now remove the toy silently, lift the cup, and ask: "How many toys are under the cup? (Zero)."
- **Number Line Start**: Point to the "0" on a ruler. Explain that before we take our first step, we start at zero.

## Questions to ask
- "Can you show me a basket with zero toys?"
- "What happens if I have 3 apples and eat all 3?"
- "Where is zero on a ruler?"
