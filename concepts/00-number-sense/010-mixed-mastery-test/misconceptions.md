# Common Misconceptions: Mixed Mastery Test

Errors during a mixed review differ from simple learning errors:

---

## 1. Context Switching Friction
* **The Mistake**: The child uses a counting strategy for a comparison question, or gets confused about what they are supposed to do because the page layout changes.
* **The Conceptual Break**: Cognitive load. Switching tasks (e.g. from counting-on to making splits of 10) takes mental effort, and children might revert to default habits.
* **How to help**: Read the prompts aloud slowly together. Give them time to adjust to the new workspace layout before they start clicking.

---

## 2. Fatiguing / Speed Rush
* **The Mistake**: The child answers the first few questions correctly but starts clicking options randomly toward the end.
* **The Conceptual Break**: Low attention span or math fatigue. 10 questions in a row can be tiring for a 5-year-old.
* **How to help**: Keep the environment quiet. Take a break mid-way if they look tired. Remember, there are no timers, so they can take as long as they need!
