# Parent Guide: Mixed Mastery Test

## What this concept means
This is a cumulative review that mixes counting, ten-frames, number lines, comparisons, and number bonds. Instead of working on one topic, the child has to switch between different types of mathematical questions.

## Why it matters
True mastery is not just solving questions right after learning a topic; it is **retention** and the ability to recall skills when mixed with others (interleaved practice). If your child passes this test with 80% or higher, they have built a solid foundation and are ready to move on to Stage 1 (Addition & Subtraction).

## How to teach using everyday objects at home
- **The Mixed Bowl**: Place a bowl of mixed toys and cards. Ask them to count, compare, and separate them randomly: "Count 4 blocks, now find the card 4. Now, if I have 5 blocks and take away 2, how many?"
- **Interleaved Play**: Rotate through the home games you played during Stage 0. Play the Fist Game, followed by a chalk walk step, then a Lego parking lot task.

## Questions to ask
- "How do you know this matches?"
- "Can you explain your thinking?"
- "Let's check the dashboard: which cards have stars?"
