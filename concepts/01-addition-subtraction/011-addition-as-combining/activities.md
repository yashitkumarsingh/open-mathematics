# Hands-on Activities: Addition as Combining

Perform these concrete games alongside the digital ten-frame activities to build physical models of addition.

---

## Game 1: The Crayon Merge (Concrete Stage)
* **Goal**: Understand part-whole relationships.
* **Items needed**: 5 blue crayons, 3 red crayons, and 2 bowls.
* **How to play**:
  1. Put the blue crayons in Bowl A and red crayons in Bowl B.
  2. Ask: *"How many blue? (5) How many red? (3)"*
  3. Tell the child: *"Now, let's merge them into a single big bowl. We are adding them together!"*
  4. Dump both sets into the big bowl. Have them count the total.
  5. Guide them to say: *"5 crayons plus 3 crayons makes 8 crayons in total!"*

---

## Game 2: Double Ten-Frame Jump (Physical Rhythmic Stage)
* **Goal**: Build visual grid aggregation.
* **Items needed**: Chalk or tape to draw a 2x5 grid on the floor.
* **How to play**:
  1. Draw a ten-frame on the floor.
  2. Place 3 blue toy blocks in the top row.
  3. Place 2 yellow toy blocks in the bottom row.
  4. Have the child hop onto each filled square to count the total, saying: *"3 blue and 2 yellow make 5 in all!"*
