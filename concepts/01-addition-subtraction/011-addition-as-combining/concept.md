# Concept Blueprint: Addition as Combining

## 1. What the Child Should Understand
- **Aggregation (Combining)**: Joining two parts together to form a larger single group. E.g., combining 3 red cars and 2 blue cars to make 5 cars in total.
- **The Plus Sign (+)**: Represents the operation of combining or joining.
- **The Equals Sign (=)**: Indicates that the amount on one side is the same as the amount on the other side.
- **Commutativity (Optional Intro)**: Combining 3 + 2 yields the same total as combining 2 + 3.

---

## 2. What the Child Should Be Able to Do
- Look at two visual sets of items and count them as a single combined group.
- Input the final sum on a ten-frame workspace showing two distinct colored counters.
- Translate a visual combination into a simple numerical equation (e.g. 4 + 3 = 7).

---

## 3. Success Criteria & Assessment Rubric
- **Level 1 (Beginning)**: Can count all items to find the sum, but needs to recount from 1 every time (counting all).
- **Level 2 (Developing)**: Can count on from the first number (e.g., seeing 4 apples and saying *"4... 5, 6, 7"*).
- **Level 3 (Mastered)**: Recognises the combined sum instantly on a ten-frame or from basic fact recall.
