# Educator Guide: Misconceptions in Addition as Combining

When children shift from basic counting to addition, they face specific conceptual traps.

---

## 1. Counting All vs. Counting On
* **The Mistake**: When combining 5 apples and 3 apples, the child counts the first set (*"1, 2, 3, 4, 5"*), then the second set (*"1, 2, 3"*), and to find the total, recounts ALL of them starting from 1 (*"1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8"*).
* **Cognitive Break**: The child does not hold the cardinality of the first set (5) in their head as a starting point. They treat addition as a single long count.
* **Correction Strategy**:
  - Cover the first set of 5 with your hand. Ask: *"How many are under here? Yes, 5. Now count on from 5 as we tap the rest: 5... 6, 7, 8."*

---

## 2. Plus Sign (+) and Equals Sign (=) Confusion
* **The Mistake**: The child writes or says "5 + 3 = 8" but cannot explain what the symbols mean, or treats "=" as "write answer here" rather than a balance.
* **Cognitive Break**: Treating equations as a recipe rather than an equivalence balance.
* **Correction Strategy**:
  - Show them a physical balance scale or draw one.
  - Explain: *"The equals sign means both sides are balanced. 5 and 3 together weigh the same as 8."*
