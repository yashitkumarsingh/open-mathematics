# Hands-on Activities: Counting On

Perform these hands-on games alongside the digital lessons to ground the concept in physical movement.

---

## Game 1: The Chalk Path Hop (Physical Space Stage)
* **Goal**: Solve starting-point confusion by hopping physically.
* **Items needed**: Chalk to draw a 0 to 10 path on the ground.
* **How to play**:
  1. Draw a number line with numbered boxes (0 to 10) on the ground.
  2. Have your child stand on the box numbered **5**.
  3. Tell them: *"I am throwing a beanbag onto +3. Can you take 3 jumps forward?"*
  4. Have them count aloud only when they land in a new box: *"1 (lands on 6), 2 (lands on 7), 3 (lands on 8)!"*
  5. Guide them to state the complete math fact: *"5 plus 3 equals 8!"*

---

## Game 2: The Tape Measure Bug Walk (Concrete Ruler Stage)
* **Goal**: Count on from the larger number.
* **Items needed**: A standard measuring tape, a small toy bug or lego figure.
* **How to play**:
  1. Roll out the tape measure on the table.
  2. Present the addition expression: $2 + 7$.
  3. Ask: *"Which number is bigger? (7). Let's put our bug on 7 first so we have fewer steps to walk!"*
  4. Place the bug on 7.
  5. Count on 2 steps: *"7... 8 (step 1), 9 (step 2)!"*
  6. Discuss why starting at 7 is much easier than starting at 2 and walking 7 times.
