# Concept Blueprint: Counting On

## 1. What the Child Should Understand
- **Cardinality Hold**: The ability to hold a starting quantity in their head (e.g., *"I have 4"*), and count forward from there (*"...5, 6, 7"*), without needing to recount from 1.
- **Direction of Value**: Moving to the right on a number line increases the value, which corresponds to the operation of addition.
- **Jump vs. Spot**: Addition matches with the number of *intervals* or hops taken, not just touching the labels.

---

## 2. What the Child Should Be Able to Do
- Locate the starting number on a number line.
- Perform a specified number of hops forward to land on the correct sum.
- Write or complete addition equations represented by visual number line hops.

---

## 3. Success Criteria & Assessment Rubric
- **Level 1 (Beginning)**: Recounts from 1 (e.g., to add 5 + 3, counts 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, then counts 6, 7, 8).
- **Level 2 (Developing)**: Starts at the first number and counts forward, but sometimes includes the starting point (e.g., starts at 5 and counts *"5, 6, 7"* instead of *"6, 7, 8"*).
- **Level 3 (Mastered)**: Confidently holds the starting number, counts forward the correct number of steps, and lands on the correct target sum.
