# Hands-on Activities: Tens and Ones

Ground the concept of place value using physical items before or alongside the digital app.

---

## Game 1: Roll and Build (Double-Digit Construction)
* **Goal**: Connect numerals to physical representations.
* **Items needed**: A Place Value Mat (drawn on paper with "Tens" and "Ones" columns), a pile of straws/pencils (units), rubber bands (to make bundles of 10), and a ten-sided die or number cards.
* **How to play**:
  1. Have the child draw a number card (e.g. **25**).
  2. Ask them to decompose the number: *"How many tens are in 25? (2). How many ones? (5)."*
  3. Have them construct the number on the mat: place 2 pre-made bundles of 10 in the **Tens** column, and 5 loose straws in the **Ones** column.
  4. Practice saying: *"2 tens and 5 ones makes 25!"*

---

## Game 2: The Ten-Bundle Race (Regrouping Basics)
* **Goal**: Practice grouping by tens.
* **Items needed**: A bowl of small items (beans, beads, or small LEGO blocks) and a standard six-sided die.
* **How to play**:
  1. Take turns rolling the die and taking that number of beans, placing them in the **Ones** column on your mat.
  2. Whenever a player gets **10 or more** individual beans, they must count out exactly 10, put them in a small cup (or baggie) to make a "Ten Bundle", and slide the cup to the **Tens** column.
  3. The first player to reach 3 Ten Bundles (30) wins!
