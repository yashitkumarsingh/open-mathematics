# Concept Blueprint: Tens and Ones (Place Value)

## 1. What the Child Should Understand
- **Group Conservations**: Ten individual ones units can be grouped/bundled into a single Ten rod, and this new unit is still worth exactly 10.
- **Place Columns**: The left digit represents how many "groups of ten" exist, and the right digit represents the "leftover ones".
- **Visual Mapping**: A vertical base-ten rod is equal to 10 unit blocks.

---

## 2. What the Child Should Be Able to Do
- Look at a representation of base-ten blocks and write or choose the correct two-digit number.
- Construct a representation of a given two-digit number by adding the correct number of Tens rods and Ones units.

---

## 3. Success Criteria & Assessment Rubric
- **Level 1 (Beginning)**: Counts rods and blocks individually by ones (e.g., counts a rod as 1, 2... 10, then blocks as 11, 12...).
- **Level 2 (Developing)**: Identifies rods as ten and blocks as one, but sometimes reverses the places (e.g., writes 32 when shown 2 tens and 3 ones).
- **Level 3 (Mastered)**: Confidently counts by tens first, then counts on the ones (e.g. *"10, 20... 21, 22, 23, 24"*), and translates blocks to two-digit numbers correctly.
