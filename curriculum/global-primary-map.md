# Global Primary Mathematics Map

This document outlines a standardized, curriculum-agnostic mathematical sequence for primary and early middle school levels. It synthesizes learning paths from around the globe (Common Core, Australian Curriculum, UK National Curriculum, Indian NCERT, and Singapore Math) to present a clean, logical foundation.

---

## The Primary Progression (Ages 4 to 12)

### Stage 0: Number Sense (Ages 4-6)
Focuses on concrete, visual representations of numbers. Building cardinality, conservation of number, spatial subitising, ordering, and part-whole structures (making 5 and 10).

* **Anchor Concepts**:
  - Counting up to 10 and 20 with 1-to-1 correspondence.
  - Understanding Zero as an empty set.
  - Subitising: instantly recognizing collections of 1–5 without counting.
  - Comparing quantities (more, fewer, same).
  - Ordering on a visual number line.
  - Visual number bonds (partitioning).

### Stage 1: Addition & Subtraction (Ages 5-7)
Transitioning from counting to combining and separating collections.

* **Anchor Concepts**:
  - Combining groups (addition) and taking away (subtraction).
  - Counting on and counting back.
  - Commutative property (e.g. 2 + 3 is the same as 3 + 2).
  - Fact families and relationships (e.g. if 4 + 2 = 6, then 6 - 2 = 4).

### Stage 2: Place Value & Multi-Digit Numbers (Ages 6-8)
Structuring numbers into tens and ones, hundreds, and beyond. Moving from base-10 manipulatives to abstract representations.

* **Anchor Concepts**:
  - Ten ones combine to make one ten.
  - Base-ten blocks, bundle sticks, and place-value charts.
  - Expanded form (e.g., 25 = 20 + 5).
  - Comparing two-digit and three-digit numbers.

### Stage 3: Multiplication & Division (Ages 7-9)
Developing multiplication as repeated addition of equal groups, and division as partitioning or sharing.

* **Anchor Concepts**:
  - Equal groups and arrays.
  - Division as sharing (how many in each group?) and grouping (how many groups?).
  - Commutative, associative, and distributive properties.
  - Fact relationships (inverses).

### Stage 4: Fractions & Decimals (Ages 8-10)
Extending the number system to parts of a whole.

* **Anchor Concepts**:
  - Partitioning shapes into equal areas.
  - Fraction notations, numerators, and denominators.
  - Equivalent fractions and number line positioning.
  - Decimals as tenths and hundredths.

### Stage 5: Measurement, Space & Data (Ages 6-11)
Connecting numbers to the physical world.

* **Anchor Concepts**:
  - Measuring length, weight, volume, and time.
  - 2D shapes, 3D solids, angles, and coordinates.
  - Data representation (tally, tables, pictograms, bar charts).
