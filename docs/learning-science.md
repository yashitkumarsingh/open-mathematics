# Learning Science & Pedagogy

The design of the Open Mathematics Foundation is based on established principles of cognitive science and mathematics education.

---

## 1. CPA: Concrete-Pictorial-Abstract
Children learn mathematics best when they progress through three stages:
1. **Concrete**: Touching and moving physical items (covered in the `parent-guide.md` hands-on activities).
2. **Pictorial**: Interacting with visual drawings, counters, ten-frames, and number lines (covered in `child.html` interactive games).
3. **Abstract**: Working with numerals, operations, and mathematical symbols (covered in practice/test modes).

## 2. Core Visual Models

### Ten-Frames
A ten-frame is a 2x5 grid. It helps children develop subitising (recognizing a count without counting individually) and places a strong emphasis on the benchmarks of **5** and **10**. For example, a child sees 7 as a full row of 5 and 2 more.

### Number Lines
Number lines represent numbers as distances from zero, helping children build a linear representation of numerical magnitude. This model is essential for understanding ordinal relationships (before, after, next) and prepares students for fractions and real number spaces.

## 3. Formative Assessment & Retention

We divide progress tracking into four specific checkpoints:
1. **Diagnostic Check**: Measures if the child already has intuitive understanding before beginning.
2. **Practice Sandbox**: Low stakes, unlimited attempts, visual hints.
3. **Mastery Check**: 8/10 or similar target correct answers under randomized setups, without hints.
4. **Retention Check**: Periodic re-testing (spaced repetition) to ensure the concept is locked into long-term memory.

## 4. Mistake Analysis
Instead of treating errors as simple failures, we categorize them into **Mistake Patterns** (e.g. "double counting", "skipping numbers", "part-whole mismatch") to help parents see exactly where the conceptual breakdown occurs.
