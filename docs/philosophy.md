# Philosophy: Why This Repository Exists

Most modern educational software focuses on the **platform** rather than the **concept**. They require user profiles, databases, online connections, and constant gamification (gems, streaks, scoreboards) to keep children hooked.

The **Open Mathematics Foundation** is built on a different set of core beliefs:

---

## 1. Concept-First, Not App-First
We structure our content by mathematical concepts, not by software features. A student should be able to play with a single mathematical idea in a clean, uncluttered sandbox without distractions.

## 2. Parent-Guided & Offline-Capable
Children learn mathematics best when they connect digital activities with off-screen play.
- Every digital concept includes a parent guide that outlines how to teach the same concept using household items (apples, blocks, fingers).
- Our entire codebase is designed to run locally off a USB drive or local disk without a server, database, or active internet connection.

## 3. High Accessibility & Zero Data Collection
Privacy is a core tenet of this framework. By keeping all progress stored inside the browser's `localStorage` and offering direct JSON export, we collect **zero** student data. There are no tracking scripts, ads, or login walls.

## 4. Calm & Playful Design
Children should feel they are playing with ideas rather than completing worksheets.
- We avoid red failure alerts that induce math anxiety.
- We do not use speed pressure or timers in early stages.
- We use gentle, pastel, and nature-inspired colors rather than flashy, over-stimulating casino-like animations.
